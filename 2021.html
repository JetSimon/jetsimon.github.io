one game a month 2021

in 2021 i challenged myself to create at least one game a month, let's see what happened!


↓

january

distanced

genre: puzzle
made for once upon a blue monday game jam

distanced is a short puzzle game where you control two people at once in two separate locations, trying to get each person to their exit.

click here to play

what i learned:

  • - how to create engaging puzzle levels that slowly teach the player mechanics
  • - how to work with Unity's tile system to make level creation a breeze
  • - creating something for a loved one is very rewarding

challenges i overcame:
  • - i found it difficult to create new levels with existing mechanics, but found creative ways to combine them in the end
  • - creating levels in such a small area, though this constraint in the end i think added to the game

things i would do different:
  • - i would make some of my code more modular, this game could be hard to expand upon without refactoring some systems
  • - level 8 feels very cheap as a concept, i would scrap this level and try and come up with something that feels better for the player

february

roadtrip

genre: arcade
made for NOKIA 3310 JAM 3

Roadtrip is a endless-obstacle-dodging-driving-game inspired by the minigames I used to love from Warioware. Can you get to 2000km?

click here to play

what i learned:

  • - how to integrate ui and art into such a small screen size
  • - customizing unity's web player for a more professional look
  • - how to work past "writer's block" and just make something

challenges i overcame:
  • - coming up with an idea that could fit the scopes of this jam was difficult for me. believe it or not this idea only came to me after about a day of throwing away too complex ones
  • - adapting ui into world space

things i would do different:
  • - i would add a few more enemies and perhaps a little more environmental variation
  • - improve pacing

march

Monstr

genre: roguelike/deckbuilder
made for 7DRL 2021

Be a lover & a fighter in this Tinder-inspired deck-building rogue-like! Can you swipe your way to level 10 and beat the Vampire Queen?

click here to play

what i learned:

  • - How to use scriptable objects with unity along with custom classes to make customization really easy!
  • - How to make a plan and stick to it! I did quite a bit of (allowed) planning before this jam to make sure I had a solid base. It really paid off!
  • - How to cut stuff - even though I had a plan I still had to cut planned content!
  • - How to make UI feel juicy.

challenges i overcame:
  • - I had to really schedule my time to balance this jam with school
  • - Figuring out a battle system that felt fairly natural to use.

things i would do different:
  • - Spend more time on testing. Some cards are quite OP and it could feel a bit better in the late game.
  • - Communicate more with my artist about scheduling. I didn't communicate enough about deadlines and therefore not all character art was finished in time for the jam.
